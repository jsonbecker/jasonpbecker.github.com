EDUC116 Syllabus

Assessment

Quizzes and Participation: 15%

There will be a weekly quiz/survey that is mandatory, but not otherwise graded. This will serve to provide me with real time feedback on the workload and pacing of the class as well as a check on understanding.

Typically, the survey will ask things like:

How long did the reading take this week? The lecture on [concept] was [interesting, boring] and [clear, unclear]. We could have spent [more,less] time on [concept] in class. Reading [X] was [interesting, boring], [clear, unclear], and [aligned, misaligned] to the material taught in class. Why are instrumental variables frequently used alongside regression discontinuity in research studies?

In addition to the weekly quiz, participation during class discussions will be assessed for this portion of your grade.

Exams: 45%

This class will have 2 take home exams. The first will be on experimental design, OLS, and random experiments. The second will be on quasi-experimental techniques. Each will review the content in the course and may involve returning to prior readings.

Final Paper 40%

For your final paper, you will be asked to review an intervention occurring right now in Rhode Island K–12 schools. This could come from the School Improvement Grant (SIG) transformation plans, other school improvement plans, Race to the Top, Race to the Top: Early Learning Challenge, or any other reform or improvement strategy you are familiar with that is occurring in Rhode Island.

Proposal 5%

A brief 1 page proposal that describes the Rhode Island intervention you’ve chosen to evaluate.

Annotated Bibliography 10%

This should include at least 5 sources that will be used in your final paper. I am requiring this so that you are certain there are sufficient existing evaluations to complete the required literature review and decrease the eventual burden of writing the final paper. My expectation is that this document will focus on methods and findings. If you have never written an annotated bibliography, please ask me about this during office hours.

Final Paper 25%

Your review should include the following:

  1. A 150 word or less abstract that summarizes the content of your paper

  2. A description of the intervention as it will be implemented in Rhode Island

    This should heavily rely on planning documents or grant applications that you have acquired and should submit with your paper. The greatest limitation on your topic choice will be the availability of these plans.

  3. A literature review of several impact evaluations of the same or similar interventions

    This review should examine the methodology used for the evaluation, describe the findings, and assess the quality of the evidence for causal impacts. The literature review should conclude with an assessment as to whether sufficient evidence existed of a causal impact for this intervention that it should be replicated in Rhode Island.

  4. An evaluation plan for the intervention

    Describe how you would design and implement a causal impact evaluation for this intervention. Detail the data that would be collected, how treatment would be assigned, the assumptions embedded in your chosen evaluation technique, and why you chose this method.
